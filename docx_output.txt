5.6 Remote Access

Only approved employees and authorized third parties (customers, vendors, etc.) shall be provided VPN clients credentials. Only one VPN access credentials will be provided. Authorized employees or third parties must adhere to following requirement:

It is the responsibility of employees/authorized party with VPN privileges to ensure that unauthorized users are not allowed access to the network.

VPN users will be automatically disconnected from network after preset minutes of inactivity. The user must then logon again to reconnect to the network.

The regular monitoring procedure involves include Port Monitoring, Ping, and Load Averages.
6.6.1 Port Monitoring
The server's primary communication services are polled every few minutes to ensure that they are online. The service is considered down if the poll was unsuccessful for the number of retries (by default 3) for any of the following:
A route to the IP address is not available.
The connection is refused.
The connection times out (by default 10 seconds unless otherwise specified).
As long as the service is online, the service will be polled every 5 minutes. Once a service is down, it will poll every 30 seconds until either the service comes back up or 5 minutes have elapsed at which that point it will go back to the default polling interval.
The services that are polled include:
HTTP (The service that serves web pages.)
POP3 (Related to email delivery.)
SMTP (Also related to email delivery.)
MySQL/MSSQL (The service that operates databases.)
SFTP (The service that manages uploading and downloading files to the server.)
SSH (The service that manages the server's shell communication.)
If one of the services listed above goes offline, alerts are generated within the server facility, and a server technician is dispatched to investigate and resolve the issue ASAP.
6.6.2 Ping
An ICMP ping request is sent to the IP address of the server every 5 minutes, requiring a response within 5 seconds. If it doesn't respond within 5 seconds, it tries every 30 seconds until the service comes back or reaches the 5 minutes mark. If it responds in time, it resumes to regular polling. Otherwise an alert is generated within the server facility, and action is taken as necessary.
6.6.3 Load Averages
Each server is pinged once per minute to retrieve its load average (how hard the server's processor is working). The load average results are displayed in the control panel used by all members of the Modular Merchant support team, and are updated every minute. If a server's load increases outside the range of acceptable performance, the appropriate action is taken immediately. The action taken will vary, depending on the cause of the high load.
The combination of monitoring and emergency procedures listed above are designed to keep all of services up and running reliably 24/7.

All related O&M actions shall start after notification from accounts to Kloud support support@kloud.com.bd

6.10 Network Latency Monitoring
The task owner shall be responsible for monitoring of all network latency and keep a log every after 3 hrs i.e. 8 times a day.
Must maintain an up-to-date database on the performance of all circuits.
Send weekly latency report to Manager for all systems.
Any systems not performing as per standard and expected, the matter must be notified through email in weekly report.
6.11 Network & System BW Graphs Monitoring
The support engineer(s) shall be held responsible for monitoring BW usages graphs.
Any unusual observation in the system must be notified through email(s).
Any new system must be kept under monitoring for at least 2 days from the date of activation.
6.12 Device Error Monitoring
Duty support engineer is to be held responsible for monitoring any device error.
The matter must be notified immediately in case of serious issue or in the case when he/she is not being able to understand the message/log etc.
In this regard, all other related SOPs shall be followed.
The task owner shall compile, raise TAC and follow up the issue till completion.
A compiled report on all device errors must be sent to Manager on daily basis.
6.13 O&M Reporting
All task owners must send his/her report(s) in time as per schedule.
All the reports must be informative and in a consolidated form.
6.14 NOC Reporting
Support engineer is to be held responsible for sending NOC report 7 days in a week basis.
It must reflect summary of all information.
The format for NOC report may get modified time to time; if so then the latest format shall be used.
6.15 SOP for Emergency
In case any emergency everyone must be ‘Cooled Down’ and work ‘Calm’.
NO other persons except mentioned below shall be in the vicinity. All such employees shall be stationed in the conference room for emergency pool in.
In case of any Emergency State - 1, Manager O&M shall hold the total control of NOC and troubleshooting activities. He/she shall be acting as ‘NOC Leader’.
In case of Emergency State – 2, Head of Planning and R&D shall hold the total control of NOC and troubleshooting activities. He/she shall be acting as ‘NOC Leader’. State – 1 ‘NOC Leader’ shall be dissolved automatically.
Following stations shall be manned:
Customer Handling
NOC Support
Customer Handling responsible shall do followings:
Get instructions from ‘NOC Leader’ regarding what to communicate to customer(s)
Receive all calls from customers
Guide support engineer as and when needed
NOC Support responsible shall do followings:
Get instructions from ‘NOC Leader’ regarding what to communicate to customer(s), if applicable
Perform normal activities
Take guidance from ‘Customer Handling’ responsible
Send emails to all clients
Take activity log with timing and reference person’s name and other details
On successful restore, all emergency states shall be dissolved and resumed to ‘Normal’
On resuming at ‘Normal’ state, the support engineer shall collect all information, compile them all and then send to all concerned internally

The task owner must maintain an up-to-date inventory status of all hardware and software.

6.19 NOC Roster
The NOC responsible must prepare a weekly roster for support duties.
The roster must be approved from HOD/Manager and then circulate internally.
The approved support roster must be maintained strictly and every assigned engineer must follow that.
Only in case of extreme urgency, the roster may be changed after approval. CEO shall be notified for any such change.
There must not be any inter-change of duties between shift engineers without prior approval from CEO/HOD/Manager O&M.
All personal problems should be kept aside and schedule must be maintained strictly.
6.20 Server Maintenance
The task owner must check all monitoring system everyday for all servers running in data center and generate a daily summary report to NOC manager.
A monthly report showing all the running server (physical and virtual) status must be sent to CEO/HOD/Manager.
All the reports must be informative and in a consolidated form.
In case of problem, T.O. shall understand its impact on services and try to address the issue accordingly.
For issues that affect customers service notify immediately – e.g. MRTG server down/gateway server down, Web server is down etc.
T.O. shall try to solve the problem by own within 10 minutes.
If that is not under his/her competence, T.O. must notify NOC Manager and take help of the concerned skilled person.
For issues that will affect on operation (later) – e.g. bad sector in hard disk, server space, database is getting full etc.
T.O. must notify NOC manager.
T.O shall contact the hardware vendor (if needed) and take a working schedule for maintenance.
Each issue must be tracked by the task owner till completion and well documented for history.
6.21 Database and Application/Site Monitoring
The task owner must check regularly that all applications (mail, dns, solarwinds, crm, mrtg, nfa, ipplan) and site (website) are running smoothly.
The task owner must submit a monthly report to CEO/HOD/Manager showing all database and application status.
Report must be informative and in a consolidated form.
In case of applications and site issues
T.O. shall contact immediately with the concerned skilled person.
In case of database issues (error/full)
T.O. must notify NOC manager.
T.O. shall take a schedule for database repair/backup.
Each issue must be tracked the by the task owner till completion and well documented for history.
6.22 Server Maintenance
Must give own weekly, monthly reports to BoD/CTO/Manager O&M as per the schedule agreed upon or as instructed time to time by management.
Manage, maintain and update relevant software and databases of the instructions and activation with reference letter/date/duration etc.
